Chicken Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

For Marinating Chicken:

  • 1kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste

For Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4-5 cups water for cooking rice
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt to taste

Other Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons ghee or cooking oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick

Biryani Masala:

  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala

Layering:

  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk
  • Fried onions
  • Ghee (optional)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well-coated. Marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or overnight for better flavor.

  2. Prepare Rice: Boil water in a large pot, add soaked rice, bay leaves, cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt. Cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked. Drain and set aside.

  3. Cook Chicken: In a large pan, heat ghee or oil. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add marinated chicken and cook until it changes color. Add tomatoes, mint, and coriander leaves. Cook until tomatoes are soft.

  4. Add Biryani Masala: Mix cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Add this biryani masala to the chicken. Cook for a few minutes until the spices are well combined.

  5. Layering: In a heavy-bottomed pan, layer half of the partially cooked rice. Spread the cooked chicken mixture over the rice. Add the remaining rice on top. Drizzle saffron-soaked milk, fried onions, and ghee on the top layer.

  6. Dum Cooking (Slow Cooking): Cover the pan with a tight-fitting lid or seal with aluminum foil. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  7. Serve: Gently mix the layers before serving to distribute the flavors. Garnish with fresh coriander, mint leaves, and fried onions. Serve hot with raita or a side salad.
