Dosa Recipe

Ingredients


For Dosa Batter:

  • 1 cup parboiled rice
  • 1 cup regular rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt to taste

For Making Dosas:

  • Oil or ghee for cooking
  • Potato masala (optional, for stuffing)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Soak and Grind: Rinse the parboiled rice, regular rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds (if using) separately. Soak them in water for 4-6 hours or overnight. Grind the soaked rice and fenugreek seeds together into a smooth batter. Grind the urad dal separately into a smooth, fluffy batter. Mix both batters together, add salt, and combine well. The batter should be of pouring consistency.

  2. Fermentation: Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become slightly fluffy.

  3. Prepare Dosa: After fermentation, mix the batter well. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to achieve the right consistency. Heat a non-stick or cast-iron dosa griddle or pan on medium heat. Grease the pan lightly with oil or ghee. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center of the pan and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer.

  4. Cooking: Cook the dosa on medium heat until the edges start to brown, and the dosa is crisp. Optionally, you can add a teaspoon of oil or ghee around the edges for a crispy texture. If you want to make a Masala Dosa, place a portion of potato masala on one half of the dosa, fold it, and serve.

  5. Serve: Repeat the process for the remaining batter. Serve dosas hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any chutney of your choice
